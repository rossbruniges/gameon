Game On Official Rules

Introduction

The objective of this contest is to encourage and promote innovation in the development of games for the open web, through the creation of games which run in Firefox and other modern Web browsers without plug-ins.

The sponsor of this Contest is Mozilla Foundation, located at 650 Castro Street, Suite 300, Mountain View, CA 94041, USA (“Sponsor”).

Minors 13 years or older who reside in the U.S. are also eligible to participate in the Contest but will be required to submit a signed parental consent form with their Entry. Email us at gameon@mozilla.com for a parental consent form.

By entering you agree to be bound by these Official Rules and all decisions of the Sponsor and judges, which are final and binding. The Contest is subject to all applicable laws.

How to Enter:

To enter the Contest, visit https://gameon.mozilla.org (the "Entry Page") and use the tools and instructions provided on the Entry Page to submit all requested information and materials, including a short description, a screenshot, and a URL where your game can be located and played online (“Entry”). Also select the category in which you are submitting your Entry. All Entry materials must be in the English language.

You may submit as many Entries as you want, each for a different game.

If you are a group of individuals submitting an Entry, the group must pick one individual who will submit the group’s Entry and be the sole recipient of any prizes for the group (in other words there is only one prize per winning Entry, not a prize for each member of the group). The names of all the individuals in the group should be noted in the description for proper attribution.

You must be the named and authorized account holder of the email address submitted in connection with your Entry. The authorized account holder is the natural person who is assigned to the email address by an internet access provider, online service provider, or other organization that is responsible for assigning email addresses or the domain associated with the submitted email address. Entries submitted will not be returned or acknowledged.

  • (f) Your game must be built using open Web technologies, such as HTML, CSS, or JavaScript (or others), as well as server-side code such as PHP, Python, Ruby or Java.

Determination of Winners

After the Contest Period has ended, all Entries will be reviewed for compliance with the Contest citeria, and then qualifying Entries will be judged by a panel of qualified judges made up of industry experts and young judges from the Hive Learning Network (the "Judges"), who will select the winners. There will be one winner and one runner up in each of the three categories listed below, and the category winner with the highest overall score will be the Grand Prize winner. The categories from which winners will be selected are: Hackable, Learning, and Multi-Device. Entrants select the category in which they want their Entry judged at the time of submission. Within each category, Entries will be judged on the following equally weighted criteria:

  1. Fun — do we enjoy playing this game?
  2. Technological achievement – how well does this game demonstrate technical mastery in open web technologies?
  3. Aesthetics – how good is the visual design and interaction experience?
  4. Originality – have we seen this before? How does the game take advantage of the web?

Each Entry will be judged by at least three Judges. Judges will not be allowed to review or rank entries from people they know personally. The Judges will give each Entry a score of 1 to 5 for each of the criteria, and the scores of all the Judges that review a particular Entry will be averaged to arrive at the final score for that Entry. The Entry with the highest score in each category will be the category winner. The Entry with the next highest score in the category will be the runner up. The Entry with the highest score overall will be the Grand Prize winner. In the event of a tie, a subset of Judges will review the tied Entries and choose the winner. The decisions of the Judges are final on all matters relating to the Contest and no correspondence will be entered into. The winners will be announced on or about the 4th of March 2013.
